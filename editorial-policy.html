Editorial policy

How AI Pilot Reviews AI SEO Tools

AI Pilot is built around a simple editorial standard: every useful article should help a reader make a clearer decision. We do not publish broad AI content unless it supports AI SEO, ecommerce SEO, or a closely related search workflow.

Source Standards

Articles should cite official documentation, primary company sources, public product pages, or reputable research when making factual claims. Claims about pricing, features, search visibility, or performance should be checked before publication and updated when the source changes.

AI Use

AI may be used for outlines, drafts, research organization, FAQ generation, and editing assistance. Final pages should still be reviewed for accuracy, search intent, usefulness, source quality, and repetition. AI-generated text should not be published without human review.

Review Criteria

Tool pages should explain who the tool is for, who should skip it, the workflow it improves, where its limits are, which alternatives to compare, and what metric proves it helped.

Update Policy

High-intent commercial pages should be refreshed when tool pricing, product positioning, major features, affiliate terms, or Google guidance changes.
